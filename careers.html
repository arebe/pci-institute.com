What Students Say About Their For-Profit Educations!

Real Testimonials -- Real Students!*

Paid fellowships, hands-on, useful training, spacious boardrooms, and more. All words our students said and all the more reason to, as one put it, “choose this school.”

Robert Brown

I received my degree from PCI. I was interested in opening a for-profit college for graphic design, and I was told they had mentors and teachers from the top for-profit art institutes. I was told I would be getting a paid fellowship at a leading graphic design university, which turned out to be an undergraduate student who had me do his laundry. Although I was expecting my advanced degree to be in business management, my diploma says associates in retail management. I was so mad I skipped my long-awaited graduation.

Nina Wallace

After a few weeks, I regretted my mistake when I decided to choose this school. The school promised hands-on and thorough training for federal finance management. Instead, the instructors were inexperienced and just handed us FAFSA forms to memorize. The training was almost all useless, rather than comprehensive as promised. I received no useful training. My specific, financial questions remained unanswered.

Richard Belding

The program I was talked into did not have access to the spacious boardrooms that convinced me to attend on a tour of the PCI campus. I was also told that students graduated ready to open their own for-profit institution, but banks laughed me back to my middle-management job when I applied for loans. The advisors and recruiters told me what I wanted to hear and sold me on their program, but really they had no idea about the program and the specific field I wanted to get into. They did not have my best interests in mind.
